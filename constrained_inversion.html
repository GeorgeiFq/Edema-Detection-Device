SWIR Phantom Inversion

1450-Anchored Constrained Inversion

Two-stage constrained inversion: (1) estimate μ_s'(1450) from the 1450 ratio as a scattering anchor, (2) sweep b and AW to fit the 1650 ratio, deriving A analytically from the anchor. Reports both AW and true total water fraction via recipe bookkeeping.
ℹ Constrained Inversion Method: This is not a full blind inversion over 3 unknowns. It is a constrained inversion that uses the 1450 ratio as a scattering anchor. The key assumption is that the 1450 far/close ratio is approximately monotonic in μ_s'(1450) and weakly dependent on water fraction. This allows μ_s'(1450) to be estimated independently, reducing the remaining search to 2 unknowns (AW, b) fitted against the 1650 ratio. The result quality depends on how valid that "water-insensitive" assumption is for your measurement regime.

02 — 1450 Anchor + Constrained Search Bounds
Stage 1 — 1450 Scattering Anchor: Scan μ_s'(1450) candidates → predict R₁₄₅₀ via forward model → collapse across AW (near-invariant assumption) → match measured R₁₄₅₀ → estimate μ_s'(1450) Stage 2 — Constrained AW/b Search: For each trial b: μ_s'(1650) = μ_s'(1450)_est × (1650/1450)^(−b) A = μ_s'(1450)_est × (1.45)^b [derived, not searched] For each trial AW: true f_w = AW + (IL stock loading) × (water fraction in IL stock) μ_a(1650) from composition predict R₁₆₅₀ → minimise E = [ln(R₁₆₅₀_meas / R₁₆₅₀_pred)]²
